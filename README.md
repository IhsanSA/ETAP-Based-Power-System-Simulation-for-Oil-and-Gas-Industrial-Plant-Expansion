⚡🛢️ ETAP-Based-Power-System-Simulation-for-Oil-and-Gas-Industrial-Plant-Expansion

This project analyzes and simulates an industrial electrical power system for an oil and gas plant expansion using ETAP software. The study includes load flow analysis, motor starting analysis, voltage drop evaluation, transformer loading, and protection coordination to assess system performance and operational reliability. Multiple industrial loads such as compressors, pumps, heaters, UPS systems, and auxiliary equipment are modeled to evaluate real operating conditions. The simulation also examines generator capacity upgrades and cable sizing adjustments to maintain voltage stability and minimize power losses during steady-state and motor starting conditions.

## Process

🏭 Studied the existing plant electrical distribution system and identified the current operational configuration.

📘 Reviewed plant expansion requirements, additional loads, and future operational demands.

⚡ Developed the single line diagram in ETAP based on the updated expansion system design.

🔌 Configured generators, transformers, cables, buses, protection devices, and industrial motor loads.

📊 Performed load flow analysis to evaluate voltage profile, transformer loading, cable loading, and system losses.

🚀 Conducted motor starting analysis for compressor and gel breaking motors to observe current, speed, and voltage stability during starting conditions.

🛡️ Designed and analyzed protection coordination using overcurrent relay, differential relay, and motor protection relay settings.

📉 Evaluated voltage drop and optimized cable sizing and transformer tap settings to improve system performance.

✅ Verified that the electrical system operated within acceptable limits for industrial oil and gas applications.

## What I Learned

- Understanding industrial power distribution systems for oil and gas facilities using ETAP.
- Performing load flow analysis to evaluate voltage profile, transformer loading, and power losses.
- Conducting motor starting simulations to analyze transient current, voltage drop, and motor acceleration performance.
- Designing protection coordination between overcurrent relay, differential relay, and motor relay systems.
- Improving cable sizing and transformer tap configuration to maintain voltage stability during plant expansion.
